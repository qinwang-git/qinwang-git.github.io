I am a senior research scientist at CSIRO Data61 and an adjunct lecturer at University of New South Wales (UNSW) and University of Technology Sydney (UTS). Before that, I had my PhD career at Swinburne University of Technology. Previously, I received my Master’s degree from Beihang University (BUAA) and Bachelor’s degree from Northwestern Polytechnical Univerisity (NPU).

My research centres on trustworthy distributed computation — how to make large-scale, decentralized systems secure, privacy-preserving, and incentive-compatible. I approach these problems through decentralized techniques (primarily blockchain), and extend the same principles to collaborative learning (e.g., split learning) and LLM-enabled systems (e.g., agentic collaboration), where security, robustness, and accountability become first-class design constraints.
